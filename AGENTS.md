## Core Rules

- [KISS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KISS_principle) — Keep It Simple.
Prefer the obvious solution. Complexity hides problems; simplicity reveals them.

- [YAGNI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/You_aren%27t_gonna_need_it) — You Aren't Gonna Need It.
Don't add it until it's needed. Understand before you build.

- [DRY](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don%27t_repeat_yourself) — Don't Repeat Yourself.
Automate the repetitive. Free your mind for what matters.

- Why, not What — Comments explain intent, not mechanics.
Document the process, not just the result.

## Style

- Language doesn't matter — clarity does. Japanese or English, whichever communicates better.
- Done > perfect. But never done without understanding.
- Leave code and people a little better than you found them.

## Working with AI

AI is a partner, not a replacement. Final judgment stays with the human.

- Be explicit about intent and constraints.
- Understand before adopting any suggestion.
- Own the output as if you wrote it yourself.
